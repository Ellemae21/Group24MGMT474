Why This Project Matters

The broader impact extends beyond technical modeling. Accurate CO predictions can support public health preparedness, enabling hospitals to anticipate spikes in admissions and allocate resources. Policymakers could use forecasts to adjust traffic timing, expand car-free zones, or issue alerts to limit outdoor activity.

Urban Air Pollution and Health: The Evolving Burden

This study explores the growing health toll of air pollution in cities and underscores the urgent need for predictive monitoring. It supports our project’s goal of using data science to inform policy before harmful exposure occurs.

Air Pollution Exposure and Respiratory Disease

Researchers found strong links between fine particulate exposure and chronic respiratory conditions, emphasizing the role of accurate forecasting in protecting vulnerable populations.

Climate Change, Urbanization, and Air Quality

This paper connects urban growth and climate dynamics to worsening air quality, highlighting how projects like ours help cities adapt through predictive modeling.

Vehicle Emissions and Urban Air Quality

Demonstrates how transportation contributes to CO levels and how traffic data can improve pollution forecasts — directly aligning with our dataset and modeling approach.

Health Impacts of Short-Term Air Pollution Exposure

Examines immediate health responses to spikes in air pollutants, supporting the need for rapid, real-time prediction tools like ours.

Mapping Air Quality for Urban Resilience

Discusses how spatial data visualization enhances city planning and public communication, reinforcing the importance of accessible, interpretable air quality data.
